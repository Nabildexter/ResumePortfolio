Directions, How to Play

You choose a buy-in of chips/money. It has to be divisible by 0.25. Everyone puts 0.25 chips/money into the pot in the beginning. Everyone will takes turns. 3 Cards are dealt on the board in a round. On your turn, you are dealt 2 cards first. You bet that the third card will be BETWEEN these two cards. When the third card is dealt and you are right, you win the amount you bet from the pot. Else, you forfeit the amount you bet if your third card was outside the range of the first two cards. Here are some caveats:

  1. If the first 2 cards dealt are the same rank, you lose automatically. You also pay half the pot (rounded down to the nearest 0.25 multiple).
  2. If the third card dealt is the same rank as any of the previous 2 cards first dealt, you lose and pay double your bet.
  3. If you ran out of chips, you are kicked from the game.
  4. You may buy back in if you go bust. Minimum $ 0.5 buy back.
  5. If the Pot is 0 after a turn, everyone automatically puts 0.25 into the pot.
  6. The Cards are Randomized. You cannot count cards. Pretend there are an infinite amount of decks shuffled together.
  7. The last person on the list will incur a tiny slight increase or decrease in buy in to account for rounding loses at the end of the game.
